## Black Rot Disease

### Overview
- **Disease Name:** Black Rot
- **Causative Agent:** *Guignardia bidwellii*
- **Impact:** Affects cultivated and wild grapes, especially destructive in warm, wet seasons.

### Affected Crops
- **Primary:** Grapes (*Vitis vinifera*)
- **Secondary:** Boston Ivy (*Parthenocissus tricuspidata*), Virginia Creeper (*Parthenocissus quinquefolia*)

### Symptoms
- **Leaves:**
  - **Early:** Small reddish-brown spots.
  - **Advanced:** Irregular reddish-brown blotches with black margins; black pycnidia in lesions.
- **Fruit:**
  - **Early:** Small, circular whitish-tan spots.
  - **Advanced:** Spots expand, berries turn black, hard, and mummified with black pycnidia.
- **Shoots and Tendrils:**
  - **Symptoms:** Oval, sunken lesions with purple to black color.

### Disease Life Cycle
- **Overwintering:** Fungi survive in canes, tendrils, leaves, and on the ground. Mummified berries are primary infection sources.
- **Infection:** Ascospores spread during rain, germinate in moisture, and penetrate plant tissues. Symptoms appear in 8 to 25 days.
- **Progression:** Continuous infection in wet conditions; pycnidia transform into pycnosclerotia and perithecia in August.

### Preventive Measures
- **Breeding for Resistance:** Develop resistant grape varieties (e.g., NY39, NY24, F25P52).
- **Chemical and Biological Control:**
  - **Novel Agent:** Uses *Toxicodendron succedaneum* leaf and *Artemisia argyi*.
- **Cultural Practices:**
  - **Pruning:** Remove infected parts, combine with fungicide.
  - **Site Selection:** Full sun, good air circulation.
  - **Spacing:** Ensure proper airflow around vines.
  - **Sanitation:** Control weeds, remove debris.

### Treatment Options
- **Chemical Treatments:** 
  - **Fungicides:** Pyraclostrobin, myclobutanil, captan, mancozeb, triadimefon, ziram.
- **Cultural Control:** Drastic pruning, debris removal.
- **Biological Control:** Use *Bacillus subtilis*, *Trichoderma harzianum*.
- **Alternative Treatments:** Plant extract agents.

### Fungicides
- **Strobilurins:** e.g., Pyraclostrobin.
- **Triazoles:** e.g., Myclobutanil.
- **Copper-Based Products:** Effective, caution with phytotoxicity.
- **Captan and Chlorothalonil:** For broad fungal control.

### Organic Treatments
- **Biological Controls:** Microbial agents, natural antagonists.
- **Cultural Practices:** Field sanitation, canopy management.

### Environmental Conditions
- **Soil pH:** Influences fungal disease; specific pH for black rot not detailed.
- **Temperature and Humidity:** Optimal for black rot:
  - **Pycnidia Production:** 20-30°C, 90-100% RH.
  - **Conidia Production:** 15-35°C, 100% RH.

### Impact on Yield
- **Fruit Loss:** Significant due to destruction and mummification.
- **Reduced Quality:** Lower fruit quality impacts market value.
- **Complete Crop Loss:** Possible in severe cases, especially in wet and warm seasons.
